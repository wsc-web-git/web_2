🎓 Summary for Students – Your Quick Revision

Mathematical Logic is the foundation of clear thinking in mathematics. In this chapter, you learned what a statement is and how we decide if it is true or false. A statement is a sentence that can be either true or false, but not both. We use small letters like p, q, r to represent statements. The truth value 'T' means true and 'F' means false.

You also saw how logic helps us build correct proofs. Every step in mathematics must follow proper reasoning. By understanding statements, you can avoid confusion and solve problems step by step. Examples like 'The sun rises in the east' are true statements, while '2+2=5' is false. Open sentences and exclamatory sentences are not statements because their truth cannot be decided.

Professor Wadhwa's approach makes logic easy. Start with simple ideas, practice with examples, and then try the Master Stroke questions. Remember, logic is not just for exams, it helps in daily life decisions, computer programming, and competitive exams like JEE and CET. Truth tables, connectives, and implications will come next, so build a strong base now.

Keep revising the truth tables, practice identifying statements daily, and always check if a sentence is really a statement. With regular practice, Mathematical Logic becomes your strength. Stay curious, keep asking why, and enjoy the beauty of exact reasoning. Revise daily, make your own examples, and connect logic to algebra and geometry for deeper understanding in JEE, CET and board exams.
